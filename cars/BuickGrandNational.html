Introduction

The Buick Grand National is a classic American muscle car that gained popularity in the 1980s. It was a high-performance version of Buick's Regal model and was known for its impressive speed and distinctive blacked-out appearance. The Grand National made its debut in 1982 as a special edition of the Buick Regal.The name "Grand National" was a nod to the NASCAR Grand National Series, where Buick had been successful.

Performance

The Grand National was powered by a turbocharged 3.8-liter V6 engine, which underwent various improvements over the years. In 1986, Buick introduced an intercooler, boosting the engine's output significantly. By 1987, the Grand National was producing around 245 horsepower and 355 lb-ft of torque.

Blackout Styling

The Grand National was known for its unique appearance, characterized by a predominantly black color scheme. Black paint, black trim, and blacked-out chrome details gave the car a stealthy and aggressive look.

Performance Figures

The Grand National was quick for its time, capable of accelerating from 0 to 60 mph in about 6 seconds. Its top speed was around 125 mph.

Popularity and Legacy

The Buick Grand National gained a cult following, especially among enthusiasts who appreciated its combination of performance and understated styling. The car's limited production numbers, especially for the GNX, contribute to its collector's appeal today.

Discontinuation

The Grand National was discontinued after the 1987 model year. Buick briefly revived the nameplate in the early 2000s for a special edition of the Regal, but it did not capture the same spirit as the original.
