Introduction

The Volvo P1800 is a classic sports car produced by the Swedish automaker Volvo. Introduced in 1961, the P1800 gained popularity for its elegant design and robust construction. The Volvo P1800 was introduced at the Brussels Motor Show in 1961 as a two-seater sports car. It was Volvo's attempt to enter the sports car market and gain a more performance-oriented image.

Engine and Performance

The early P1800 models were powered by a 1.8-liter, four-cylinder engine, producing around 100 horsepower. Over the years, the engine and performance were improved, with later models featuring larger engines and more power.

Construction

The P1800 was constructed with a sturdy steel body and used components from other Volvo models for reliability. Production of the bodies initially began at the British Jensen Motors and later moved to Sweden.

Design

The P1800 was designed by Pelle Petterson, who was working at the Italian design firm Frua at the time. The car features a sleek and timeless design, with a long hood, distinctive front grille, and curved rear glass.

End of Production

Production of the P1800 series continued until 1973 when it was succeeded by the Volvo 1800ES. In total, approximately 39,414 units of the P1800 series were produced.
